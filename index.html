Welcome to ICACCCN - 2018

Galgotias Educational Institutions (GEI) comprising of Galgotias University (GU), Galgotias College of Engineering & Technology (GCET), Galgotias Institute of Management & Technology (GIMT) and Galgotias Business School (GBS) were founded by Smt. Shakuntla Educational and Welfare Society.

The GCET are approved by AICTE, Ministry of HRD, Government of India and affiliated to Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam Technical University, Lucknow (U.P.). The Institution offers 9 Under Graduate Programme in Regular and 5 PG Programme in Full Time. The Campus is located on 8-lane expressway connecting Greater Noida with New Delhi and is surrounded by a green landscape and lush environment.

The Information Technology & Engineering Department is one of the Departments of GCET that came in to existence in 2000. The department has grown significantly after its inception and currently offers a UG program in Information Technology and Engineering which enriches the knowledge of Practical Engineers from the nearby Industries and Research Institutions which includes Government and Private Sectors utilizing the locational opportunity of National Capital Region.

International Conference on Advances in Computing, Communication Control and Networking –ICACCCN (ICAC3N'18) will be held during October 12-13, 2018 in Greater Noida, India, Galgotias College of Engineering and Technology.

The conference is an international forum which aims to bring together leading academician, researchers and research scholars to exchange and share their experiences and hard-earned technological advancements about all aspects of based on their research related to Computing, Communication Control & Networking.

We invite all leading researchers, engineers and scientists in the domain of interest from around the world. We warmly welcome all authors to submit your research papers to ICAC3N'18, and share the valuable experiences with the scientist and scholars around the world.

Nearest Tourist Places

Taj Mahal
Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum located on the southern bank of the Yamuna River in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife of three, Mumtaz Mahal.

Red Fort
India Gate

The Red Fort was the residence of the Mughal emperor of India for nearly 200 years, until 1857. It is located in the centre of Delhi and houses a number of museums.

India Gate
India Gate

At the centre of New Delhi stands the 42 m high India Gate, an "Arc-de-Triomphe" like archway in the middle of a crossroad. Almost similar to its French counterpart, it commemorates the 70,000 Indian soldiers who lost their lives fighting for the British Army during the World War I. The memorial bears the names of more than 13,516 British and Indian soldiers killed in the Northwestern Frontier in the Afghan war of 1919.

Lotus Temple
Lotus Temple

East of Nehru place, this temple is built in the shape of a lotus flower and is the last of seven Major Bahai's temples built around the world. Completed in1986 it is set among the lush green landscaped gardens.

Akshardham
Akshardham

Swaminarayan Akshardham at New Delhi is a Mandir – an abode of God, a Hindu house of worship, and a spiritual and cultural campus dedicated to devotion, learning and harmony.

Qutab Minar
Qutab Minar

Qutab Minar is a soaring, 73 m-high tower of victory, built in 1193 by Qutab-ud-din Aibak immediately after the defeat of Delhi's last Hindu kingdom. The tower has five distinct storeys, each marked by a projecting balcony and tapers from a 15 m diameter at the base to just 2.5 m at the top. The first three storeys are made of red sandstone; the fourth and fifth storeys are of marble and sandstone. At the foot of the tower is the Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque, the first mosque to be built in India.
